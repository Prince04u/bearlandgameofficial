Inside Survival on Bear Land

Classic survival, modernized. Towns, land claims, balanced economy, friendly community.

The heart of Bear Land

Survival is the spiritual centre of the network. It's where most of us started, where our oldest players still log in every day, and where the community is at its warmest. If you want classic Minecraft survival with quality-of-life improvements but without the pay-to-win baggage of most large networks, this is your home.

The world is freshly seeded with custom biomes, larger ore veins than vanilla, and a hand-built spawn city that newcomers can use as a market, a meeting place and a temporary home.

Land claiming made simple

We use a golden-shovel claim system. Right-click two corners and your build is protected forever — from griefers, from explosions, from creepers, from absolutely everything you didn't explicitly trust. Trusting friends is a single command. Removing trust is just as fast.

Every account starts with 500 claim blocks and earns more for playtime. BearPrime members and store ranks get bonus blocks. There is no auto-decay on claims as long as the owner logs in monthly, so your base is safe even if you take a break.

Towns and nations

Towns are the next step. Found a town, invite players, set tax rates, build civic infrastructure. Towns can declare alliances, host markets, and even merge into nations for cooperative builds across regions of the map. Some of our oldest towns have been continuously inhabited for over four years.

Town leaders get extra moderation tools for their own territory — they can set local rules, ban troublemakers from town land, and customize a town spawn point. We do not interfere unless the network rules are broken; how your town is run is up to you.

Economy and jobs

The economy runs on BearCoin. Earn it by working jobs (mining, farming, fishing, hunting, building), by selling at player shops, by completing daily quests, or by trading directly with other players. Prices are player-set; we do not manipulate them, and there are no admin shops competing with you.

Jobs scale gently so new players can earn meaningful income from their first hour without making veteran economies obsolete. We rebalance weekly based on transaction data to prevent runaway inflation.

Why people stay

The single thing every long-time Survival player tells us is that the community is what keeps them. Helpful chat, low toxicity, an active staff team and regular events make it feel less like a server and more like a small town you actually want to come home to.

If you want a survival world that respects your time, protects your builds, and gives you a real economy to participate in, jump in. Spawn is open. The mayor will probably say hi.
